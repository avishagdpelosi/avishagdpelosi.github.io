Research Vision

My work asks how conscious experience becomes structured, how meaning becomes intrinsic to experience, and how agency arises within a field of possibilities. I approach these questions through formal systems thinking, neuroscience, contemplative and phenomenological inquiry, and the study of human transformation.

About

I am a faculty member in Mechanical Engineering at Braude College of Engineering. My background includes aerospace engineering, biomechanics, neuroscience-oriented experimental work, psychotherapy, and contemplative practice. My current research brings these perspectives together to investigate the foundations of consciousness, meaning, agency, and transformation.
